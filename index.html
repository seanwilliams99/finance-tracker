💡 ISA & LISA — First-Time Buyer Tips
You can each save up to £20,000/year in an ISA (tax-free). For a first home, a Lifetime ISA (LISA) gives a 25% government bonus on up to £4,000/year — that's up to £1,000 free per person per year. You must be under 40 to open one. Funds can be used on a home worth up to £450,000. Consider a Cash ISA for the remainder. Note: interest on a regular (non-ISA) savings account above your Personal Savings Allowance (£1,000/year for basic-rate taxpayers, £500 for higher-rate) is taxable — so a low-interest regular saver loses out twice, on rate and potentially on tax.
